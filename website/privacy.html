Privacy Policy

Your privacy is our top priority

TL;DR: GrammarGuard collects ZERO personal data. Everything runs locally on your device. We don't track you, we don't store your writing, and we don't have servers to collect your information. Your privacy is 100% protected.

1. Information We DON'T Collect

Unlike most online services, GrammarGuard is designed to collect zero personal information. Here's what we explicitly do NOT collect:

  • Your name, email, or personal information
  • The text you write or edit
  • Your browsing history
  • Your IP address
  • Cookies or tracking data
  • Usage analytics or telemetry
  • Device fingerprints
  • Location data
  • Any other personally identifiable information

2. How GrammarGuard Works

GrammarGuard is a completely local, client-side application:

  • Local Processing: All grammar and spelling checking happens entirely on your device using JavaScript
  • No Server Uploads: Your text never leaves your computer or uploads to any server
  • Offline Functionality: Works completely offline without any internet connection
  • Chrome Storage API: Settings and achievements are stored locally in your browser using Chrome's Storage API
  • No External Requests: The extension makes zero network requests or API calls

3. Data Storage

GrammarGuard stores some data locally on your device to provide functionality:

What We Store Locally:

  • Extension Settings: Your preferences (dark mode, language selection, etc.)
  • Custom Dictionary: Words you've added to your personal dictionary
  • Achievements: Your writing stats, streak count, and unlocked achievements
  • Writing Goals: Your personal daily word goals and progress

Important Notes:

  • All data is stored using Chrome's local Storage API
  • Data never syncs to the cloud (unless you enable Chrome sync, which is controlled by Google, not us)
  • You can clear all data at any time from the extension settings
  • Uninstalling the extension removes all stored data

4. Permissions Explained

GrammarGuard requests certain Chrome permissions. Here's exactly what each permission is used for:

  • activeTab: To check grammar on the page you're currently viewing
  • storage: To save your settings and achievements locally
  • contextMenus: To add right-click menu options
  • notifications: To show achievement notifications (optional, can be disabled)

We do NOT request permission for:

  • Reading all your tabs or browsing history
  • Making network requests
  • Accessing your camera, microphone, or location
  • Reading files from your computer

8. Your Rights

Since we don't collect any personal data, there's nothing for us to access, share, or delete on our end. However, you have full control:

  • Right to Access: All your data is stored locally on your device - you can access it anytime through Chrome's Developer Tools
  • Right to Delete: Clear all data from extension settings or by uninstalling
  • Right to Export: Export your custom dictionary and settings from the extension
  • Right to Opt-Out: Simply uninstall the extension if you choose not to use it

9. Children's Privacy

GrammarGuard does not collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13. Since we collect zero data, we are compliant with COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) and similar regulations worldwide.
